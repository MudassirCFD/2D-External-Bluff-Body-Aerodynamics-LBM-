# Results Summary

This file gives the main numerical results from the circle, square and droplet LBM wake comparison.

All three bodies were compared at the same Reynolds number, same domain, same inlet speed and same post processing route.

## Main results

| Body | Mean Cd | RMS Cl | Strouhal estimate |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Circle | 1.506 | 0.542 | 0.210 |
| Square | 1.698 | 0.448 | 0.174 |
| Droplet | 1.181 | 0.229 | 0.203 |

## Main comparison

The droplet reduced mean drag by about 30.5 per cent compared with the square.

The droplet reduced mean drag by about 21.6 per cent compared with the circle.

The droplet also gave the lowest RMS lift coefficient, which means the unsteady side force from vortex shedding was weaker in this run.

## Engineering reading

The square produced the highest drag because the sharp corners forced early separation and created a wide wake.

The circle gave a cleaner wake than the square, but it still behaved as a bluff body with a clear vortex street and strong unsteady lift.

The droplet gave the best result in this setup because the tapered rear helped the flow recover behind the body, narrowed the wake and reduced both mean drag and lift fluctuation.

## Note

These values are from a numerical comparison study. They should not be treated as final validation grade results until grid independence, domain sensitivity, outlet sensitivity and benchmark circular cylinder comparison are completed.
